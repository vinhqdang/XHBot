\section{Discussion}
The profound performance gap between standard homophilic frameworks and XHBot underscores a critical shift required in adversarial graph learning. While prior models rely heavily on node feature distinctness, real-world botnets are continuously evolving their profiles to mimic humans. By shifting the detection burden to structural isolation (via SGTR) and identity preservation (via THCA), XHBot maintains robustness even under high-noise feature overlaps. Furthermore, the inclusion of the HFE module bridges the gap between theoretical classification and practical enforcement, allowing moderators to query the subgraph rationale behind any ban recommendation.
